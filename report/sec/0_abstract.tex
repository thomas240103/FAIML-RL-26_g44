\begin{abstract}
This report studies reinforcement learning for continuous control and sim-to-real transfer in the simplified sim-to-sim setting proposed by the project. First, we implemented REINFORCE, REINFORCE with a constant baseline, and an actor-critic policy-gradient variant from scratch on \texttt{Hopper-v4}. Then, we moved to the \texttt{PandaPush-v3} manipulation task and trained Stable-Baselines3 PPO and SAC agents under source and target dynamics, where the target object is heavier than the source object. The final implementation includes an evaluation pipeline for fixed and randomized object mass and friction, a reward-shaping wrapper, Uniform Domain Randomization (UDR) over mass and friction, and a mass-only Adaptive Domain Randomization (ADR) wrapper. On the currently available deterministic 50-episode SAC evaluations, the source-trained SAC policy reaches 98\% success on source and 96\% success on target at the nominal target mass, while the target-trained oracle reaches 100\% on target. Under a harder randomized evaluation with mass sampled in $[5,10]$ kg and randomized friction, the source-trained non-randomized SAC policy drops to 76\% success, whereas the UDR policy trained with mass and friction randomization reaches 90\%. PPO, ADR, multi-seed statistics, and the final Part 1 numerical table are not yet complete and are explicitly marked as TODO where needed.
\end{abstract}
